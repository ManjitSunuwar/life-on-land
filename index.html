About the Topic

Agriculture is the backbone of Nepal's economy, supporting the livelihoods of over 60% of the population. Wheat and rice are the two most critical staple crops produced across diverse ecological zones.

This project examines how crop production has evolved over five years in three provinces — analyzing area harvested, total production in metric tons, and yield efficiency — to provide data-driven insights into agricultural performance and long-term sustainability.

📄 DBSN-S2026

About the Project

A structured database design study of Nepal's agricultural crop production

📅

Study Period

2019 – 2023 (5 years of annual crop production records)

📍

Geographic Scope

Three provinces of Nepal: Lumbini, Madesh Province, and Sudurpashchim

🌿

Crops Analyzed

Wheat and Rice — Nepal's two primary staple food crops

Project Objectives

Demonstrating database design principles with real agricultural data

  1. Design and implement a relational database for crop production data across Nepal provinces.
  2. Analyze wheat and rice production trends from 2019 to 2023 across three provinces.
  3. Compare agricultural output across Lumbini, Madesh Province, and Sudurpashchim.
  4. Calculate and visualize yield efficiency (MT per Hectare) per crop and province.
  5. Provide data-driven insights into sustainable land use and agricultural productivity.

Provinces Covered

Three provinces selected as major agricultural zones of Nepal

Lumbini Province

Largest wheat-producing province; significant rice output with the highest cultivated wheat area at 515,000 Ha in 2023.

Madesh Province

Highest rice output and best wheat yield efficiency; Terai lowlands support premium paddy cultivation conditions.

Sudurpashchim Province

Consistent growth in both crops; shows strong untapped potential despite lower current production volumes.

Data Source

The crop production data used in this project is derived from official agricultural statistics of Nepal, covering annual records for wheat and rice across three provinces. Data includes area harvested in hectares (Ha), total production in metric tons (MT), and yield efficiency expressed as MT per Ha. The dataset spans five consecutive years (2019–2023), providing a meaningful time-series for trend analysis.

📝 Final Summary

Conclusion

Key findings, insights, and recommendations from DBSN-S2026

Project Summary

The DBSN-S2026 project successfully applied database design principles to model, store, and analyze five years of wheat and rice crop production data from three provinces of Nepal: Lumbini, Madesh Province, and Sudurpashchim. Using a structured relational schema, the data was organized into logical entities — crops, provinces, and production records — enabling efficient querying, aggregation, and visualization.

The analysis demonstrated how database-driven approaches can transform raw agricultural data into actionable insights. Bar chart visualizations revealed clear trends in production volumes, harvested areas, and yield efficiencies, providing a strong foundation for evidence-based agricultural decision-making.

Key Findings

Insights from the five-year crop production analysis

1

Consistent Growth Across All Provinces

Both wheat and rice production showed steady year-on-year increases from 2019 to 2023 across all three provinces, demonstrating improved agricultural productivity throughout Nepal.

2

Lumbini Leads in Wheat Production

Lumbini Province consistently produced the highest wheat output, reaching 1,120,000 MT in 2023 — the largest cultivated area of 515,000 Ha among all three provinces.

3

Madesh Province Dominates Rice Output

Madesh Province had the highest rice production, peaking at 1,530,000 MT in 2023, benefiting from favorable lowland ecology and extensive paddy cultivation.

4

Madesh Has the Best Wheat Yield Efficiency

Despite having the smallest wheat cultivation area, Madesh Province achieved the highest yield of 3.29 MT/Ha, indicating superior crop management and soil conditions.

5

Rice Yields Are Comparable Across Provinces

Rice yield efficiency (4.05–4.25 MT/Ha) was notably uniform across all provinces, suggesting that rice is well-adapted to Nepal's diverse agro-climatic conditions.

6

Sudurpashchim Shows Steady Growth Potential

Though currently having the lowest output, Sudurpashchim exhibited consistent upward trends in both wheat and rice, indicating significant untapped agricultural potential.

Recommendations

  1. Invest in irrigation infrastructure in Sudurpashchim Province to boost yield efficiency and expand cultivated area for both wheat and rice.
  2. Replicate Madesh Province's wheat farming practices — particularly seed selection and soil management — in provinces with lower yield rates.
  3. Leverage database-driven monitoring systems to track seasonal crop performance in real-time across all provinces.
  4. Promote crop diversification alongside wheat and rice to reduce dependency on two crops and improve long-term soil health.
  5. Develop provincial agricultural policies informed by yield trend analysis to better meet Nepal's growing food demand.

Closing Remarks

This project illustrates the power of structured data management in understanding agricultural trends. The relational database model proved effective in organizing and querying multi-dimensional crop data across time and geography.

The upward production trends observed from 2019 to 2023 are encouraging signs of agricultural resilience in Nepal. However, disparities in yield efficiency between provinces highlight the need for targeted interventions and knowledge transfer.

With proper data infrastructure and continued monitoring, Nepal's agricultural sector has strong potential to improve food security, land-use efficiency, and rural livelihoods — directly contributing to sustainable life on land.
